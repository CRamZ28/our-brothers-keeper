# A Note from the Founder

---

I want to tell you why I built this, because I think it matters before you look at any of the code.

My brother passed away and left behind a wife and three young boys.

In the days and weeks after, people showed up. The way people do when something devastating happens — with meals, and phone calls, and presence, and prayer. It was real. It was genuine. And then, as life does, it slowly faded.

Not because those people stopped caring.

Because caring without structure is hard to sustain. Because memory fades. Because everyone's life kept moving forward while grief didn't. I watched the phone get quieter. I watched my sister-in-law navigate an enormous amount of need with a shrinking amount of visible support. I watched three boys grow up with their father gone and their community, bit by bit, unintentionally drift away.

And I kept thinking: this is a solvable problem.

Not the grief. Grief isn't a problem you solve. But the forgetting — the structural failure of community support — that's a problem worth working on.

So I started building.

---

I should tell you something important: I'm not a developer. I didn't know how to code when I started this.

I built Our Brother's Keeper using AI tools — primarily Replit's AI agent — because I had a clear vision of what this needed to be and I wasn't willing to let the absence of a technical background be the reason it didn't exist. I learned as I went. I pushed through the parts I didn't understand. I iterated until it looked and felt like what I saw in my head.

And then I reached the edge of what I could do alone.

The app works. The mission is real. But to get from where it is to where it needs to be — hardened, accessible, reliable, and eventually on mobile — I need people who actually know what they're doing.

That's why this repository is open.

---

I want to be direct about what I'm asking for.

This is not a startup looking for technical co-founders. This is not a growth product looking for engineers to help it scale. This is a tool built to protect widows and children from being forgotten, and I'm opening it to the community because the mission is bigger than my skillset, and because I believe that's the right thing to do.

If you contribute to this project, you're not building someone's SaaS product. You're building something that a widow somewhere will use on a Tuesday afternoon in year two of her grief, when the world has largely moved on, to see that someone still remembered to bring dinner.

That matters. I hope you feel that.

---

A few things I want you to know before you open a pull request:

**The faith foundation is real, and it's not going away.** The name is a reference to Scripture. The verse at the bottom of the landing page is there on purpose. I built this because of a conviction that we are called to carry one another's burdens. I won't apologize for that, and I won't hide it.

But the welcome is genuine too. This platform is for anyone who wants to love a grieving family well, regardless of their belief. If you contribute code, I don't care what you believe. I care that you understand the people this serves.

**The code has AI fingerprints on it.** Some of it is solid. Some of it will need a thoughtful developer's eye to refactor and harden. I know that. I'm not asking you to ignore it. I'm asking you to see it for what it is — a passionate non-developer proving out a concept — and help carry it forward.

**Privacy is non-negotiable.** This platform handles data from families in the hardest circumstances of their lives. Every feature should be held against that reality.

---

The first thing I'd ask any contributor to do is read [VISION.md](VISION.md) and [PHILOSOPHY.md](PHILOSOPHY.md). Not because I expect you to agree with everything, but because the people this is built for deserve to have their interests represented by the people building it.

If the mission resonates, I'm glad you're here.

If you see ways to make this better, I need that.

If you know someone who has walked through this kind of loss and wants to see this exist in the world — share it with them.

---

Caleb Ramsey
*Founder, Our Brother's Keeper*
